LSTM versus MLP

Feb 20, 2017

Playing around with RNN and LSTM for time series modelling so far resulted in disappointment. Traditional MLPs seem to perform better. On the internet RNNs are often pitched for time-series data, so what am I doing wrong?

I decided to analyse performance of LSTM time-series forecasting systematically.

First I needed to find suitable experimental data with a time dependent feature set and which can be controlled in an experimental setup easily.

Reading http://simaaron.github.io/Estimating-rainfall-from-weather-radar-readings-using-recurrent-neural-networks/ was enlightening and helpful:

Part 1: The Adding Problem

The prediction of cumulative values from variable-length sequences of vectors with a ‘time’ component is highly reminiscent of the so-called Adding Problem in machine learning—a toy sequence regression task that is designed to demonstrate the power of recurrent neural networks (RNN) in learning long-term dependencies (see Le et al. , Sec. 4.1, for a recent example):


Braced with this data I started my experiments here.

Conclusion

Traditional MLP models seem to be better suited for this kind of sequence problem. They converge for sequence sizes > 50 and seem to have lower MSE. This leaves me wondering whether the prevalent opinion on the internet on RNNs and especially LSTMs for time series data modelling seems to be misguided.
